The Fault in Our Stars is a young adult fiction novel by John Green, published in 2012. The novel tells the story of Hazel Grace Lancaster, a 16-year-old girl with thyroid cancer that has spread to her lungs, and Augustus Waters, a 17-year-old boy with osteosarcoma. Hazel and Augustus meet at a cancer support group and fall in love.

Despite their illnesses, Hazel and Augustus live a full and meaningful life together. They travel to Amsterdam to meet Hazel's favorite author, Peter Van Houten, and they experience the joys and sorrows of first love. Hazel and Augustus also learn to deal with the reality of their illnesses and the inevitability of death.

The Fault in Our Stars is a moving and heartwarming story about the power of love and the importance of living life to the fullest. It is a reminder that even in the face of death, there is always hope and beauty to be found.

**Summary of the plot:**

The novel begins with Hazel Grace Lancaster attending a cancer support group at the behest of her parents. Hazel is a cynical and withdrawn teenager who is obsessed with death. At the support group, she meets Augustus Waters, a charming and optimistic boy who has lost his leg to cancer.

Hazel and Augustus quickly fall in love. They share a passion for books, and they bond over their shared experiences with cancer. Augustus gives Hazel a new lease on life, and she helps him to see the world in a new light.

Augustus uses his Make-A-Wish wish to take Hazel on a trip to Amsterdam to meet her favorite author, Peter Van Houten. However, the trip turns out to be a disappointment, as Van Houten is a cruel and disillusioned man.

Despite the setbacks, Hazel and Augustus continue to love and support each other. Augustus's cancer progresses rapidly, and he eventually dies. Hazel is devastated, but she finds comfort in the memories of their time together.

The Fault in Our Stars is a story about love, loss, and hope. It is a reminder that even in the face of death, there is always beauty and meaning to be found.
